Graduate Program

The Accelerated BS/MS program offers a streamlined pathway for high-achieving SUNY Korea TSM undergraduate students to earn both their Bachelor's and Master's degrees within five years by counting graduate-level courses taken during their senior year toward both degrees.

Anyone who has questions regarding the program, please contact the department graduate program director.

📌 Eligibility Requirements

  • Current Major: Technology and Society Undergraduate Students.
  • Current Academic Standing: Last semester of Junior year.
  • Cumulative GPA: 3.00 or higher at the time of application.
  • Prerequisite Coursework: EST 202, EST 391, and EST 393 (combined GPA on these three courses ≥ 3.25)
  • Timeline Constraint: Students must have two semesters remaining to complete graduate courses.

📁 Required Documentation

  • Curriculum Vitae (CV): Detailed research and academic profile.
  • Statement of Purpose: Please include the following information:
    • Max. 3 pages detailing relevant experience,
    • Goals: short-term, long-term
    • Wanted accomplishments with MS.
  • Transcript: Most recent unofficial academic transcript.
  • Recommendation Letters: At least two letters are required. * At least one letter must be from a current DTS faculty member, sent directly to the department.

📚 Key Accelerated Pathway Courses

To fulfill the accelerated transition smoothly, students transition into core TSM graduate courses during their remaining semesters:

The MS program prepares students for leadership in technology, policy, and systems-based organizations.

Anyone who has questions regarding the program, please contact the department graduate program director.

📌 Eligibility Requirements

  • Degree: Bachelor's degree in engineering, science, mathematics, policy, or a related field.
  • GPA: Minimum undergraduate GPA of 3.00.
  • Letters: Three letters of recommendation.
  • English: TOEFL minimum 85 / IELTS overall 6.5 (no band below 6.0).

📁 Required Documentation

  • Curriculum Vitae (CV): Detailed research and academic profile.
  • Statement of Purpose: Academic goals, research interests, and career plans.
  • Transcripts: Undergraduate transcripts (only official is allowed).
  • Recommendations: At least three letters, including one from academic faculty.

🎓 Master’s Project Requirement

Develop an applied project with faculty-supervised deliverables.

The PhD program in Technology, Policy, and Innovation is designed for researchers in socio-technical systems, technology governance, and innovation policy.

If student joins the program, the student will be required to visit Stony Brook University (SBU) to complete their required courses (SOC/POL: 501 &502) for Part B and prepare for Part A.

If the student earned 21 credits toward their PhD program, the 7-year-time-limit will begin and the student will be required to complete their PhD within the time.

Anyone who has questions regarding the program, please contact the department graduate program director.

📌 Eligibility Requirements

  • Degree: Bachelor's degree in engineering, science, mathematics, policy, or a related field.
    • *Note: Master's degree is not required for TPI PhD program. Direct PhD is available.
  • GPA: Minimum undergraduate GPA of 3.00 and strong academic preparation.
  • Letters of Recommendation: Three academic or research references.
  • Research Preparedness: Evidence of prior academic research experience or relevant project work.
  • English: TOEFL minimum 85 / IELTS overall 6.5 (no band below 6.0).

📁 Required Documentation

  • Curriculum Vitae (CV): Detailed research and academic profile.
  • Statement of Purpose: Research objectives and academic fit with the department.
  • Official Transcripts: Undergraduate and any graduate records.
  • Letters of Recommendation: Three letters from faculty or research supervisors. * At least one letter must be from academic field.

🔬 Areas of Concentration

Focus Area Description
Technology Policy & Innovation Research on regulation, governance, and the social impacts of emerging technologies.

📝 Doctoral Milestones & Exams

1. Parts B (@ SUNY Korea & SBU)

Achieve and complete a 3.70 average in required courses:
• EST 600, EST 610, EST 625, SOC 501 or POL 501, SOC 502 or POL 502
• SOC/POL 501 & 502 are not offered at SUNY Korea. Students are required to visit SBU for those courses.

Note 1: Students must pass Part B first and then proceed to Part A.

Note 2: Students must maintain a minimum letter grade of A- on all Part B courses.

2. Part A (@ SBU)

Submit a written report and present original research by the fourth semester.

3. Dissertation Proposal Defense (@ SBU & SUNY Korea)

Defend a written research proposal within 18 months of the qualifying exam.

4. Advancement to Candidacy (@ SUNY Korea)

Advance to G5 status and maintain active research registration.

🎓 Graduation & Residency Requirements

  • Dissertation Committee: During SBU visiting, student is recommended to form a dissertation committee. Generally, the committee members should be included 2 from SBU, 2 from SUNY Korea, at least 1 from external institution. Advising faculty of the student is not allowed to be a committe chair.
  • Teaching Requirement: Taking a 'Teaching or Practicum Experience' is required for graduation.
  • SBU Residency: Complete at least two consecutive semesters at SBU as full-time is required for the program.
