Undergraduate Program

Program Overview

Technological Systems Management (TSM) prepares graduates to lead digital initiatives and manage major projects in fast-paced global environments of technological change and disruption.

120+ Total Credits
39 Upper-Division Credits
~79 Major Requirements

• Upper-Division Credits can be earned by taking 3-400 lv. courses regardless of department.
• Upper-Division Credits will also count toward as total credits.

Areas of Specialization

Students must complete 1 specialization track (about7 related courses, min 21 credits total).

Track Highlights
Information and Communication Technology for Development (ICT4D) Focuses on the application of ICT in global sustainable development frameworks. (SUNY Korea specialized track)
Computer Science (CSE) Combines rigorous software engineering and database skills with technology policy. It empowers students to leverage core IT structures to build smart cities, manage digital networks, and solve complex global challenges.
Applied Math & Stats (AMS) Engages with quantitative structures, statistical data modeling, and advanced decision analytics.

Stony Brook University Visit Requirement

Visiting Stony Brook University for 2 semesters is required for graduation.

Requirements for visiting SBU

  • Prerequisites: C or higher in Calculus I(AMS151 or MAT131) and the WRT 101 course.
  • Minimum GPA: Cumulative GPA of 2.0 or higher is strictly required.
  • Academic Standing: Sophomore year.

Major Transfer

Criteria for Non-CEAS students transferring to TSM, or AOI pre-majors declaring the formal major.

Requirements for Acceptance into the Major

  • Prerequisites: C or higher in Calculus I, Calculus II, and the SNW course.
  • Minimum GPA: Cumulative GPA of 2.80 or higher is strictly required.
  • Time Limit: All requirements must be met within 1 year of enrolling in the first major-entry course.
  • Note: Fulfilling requirements does not guarantee entry; admission is competitive and subject.
